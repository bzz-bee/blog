My openSUSE Experience

March 2 2026

openSUSE (they don't capitalize the 'o') is a pretty good distro provided you already have linux experience. It's not that hard to adjust to, but YaST can be confusing at first. You could also just not use YaST GUI and change settings and install packages in the terminal (and feel like a hacker lol). I came from 3 years on Linux Mint, tried other Debian based distros here-and-there, and I was wondering if I would like a non-Debian distro. I was researching a lot. You never want to jump in to a new distro blindly, in my opinion. This video was really helpful:

  • "Why I love openSUSE"- The Linux Cast

    • I tried it and I liked it, especially the default colors and the chameleon mascot. Some say people only use openSUSE for that, and well, there might be some truth in that, heh. openSUSE Tumbleweed is a rolling-release, like Fedora. I think that's neat, hasn't gone wrong for me. The zypper package manager is decent. Decent repositories. Could be faster, but it's fine.

    The desktop environment I chose was XFCE. I like the rat mascot. I felt Gnome was too tablet-like and ugly, KDE too flashy and modern looking. They aren't bad environments by any means, they come with great tools, just not my taste. So I settled on openSUSE in July 2025. I encountered issues with the display manager, lightdm was the only one that worked, and it was good enough. At this point I was unfamiliar with tiling window managers, funny how I would try to tile windows manually and I set up keybinds for moving a window to the top left, top, top right, so on. I didn't know how much I needed/wanted a tiling wm.

    I finally got tired of XFCE and looked into tiling wms. When I tried Sway, I realized xinput didn't work on Wayland, which I relied on to fix my glitched laptop keyboard (by disabling it completely). Luckily I figured out how to do that on Wayland. But Sway just wasn't working for me. Then I tried Hyprland, and spent 3 days ricing. I use Waybar, I like how it uses css.

    Unfortunately, the YaST GUI doesn't work in Wayland. At least the TUI does, but its blue background and white text is hard to look at. Network Manager TUI (nmtui) has a blue background too but at least the actual menu background is grey with black text.
